Abstract

Despite India's Aadhaar program enrolling over 1.3 billion residents, significant exclusion zones persist, particularly affecting vulnerable populations in remote and tribal areas. This research presents a comprehensive data-driven analysis of Aadhaar enrollment patterns across 1,045 districts and 49 states/UTs, identifying critical gaps in coverage.

Using advanced machine learning techniques (Gradient Boosting Classifier with 99.04% accuracy), we developed a risk prediction model that successfully identifies high-exclusion districts based on demographic, geographic, and biometric authentication patterns. Our analysis reveals that 174 districts (16.7%) exhibit high exclusion risk, with children aged 0-5 years disproportionately affected.
